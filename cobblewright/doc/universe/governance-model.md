🪐 20. Wright Universe Governance Model (Canonical Draft v1.0)
/docs/universe/governance-model.md

1. Purpose
Define the universal laws, safety rules, capsule constraints, and governance principles that apply across all realms in the Wright Universe.

This ensures the cosmos remains:

coherent

safe

capsule‑aligned

explainable

expandable

2. Governance Principles
2.1 Player Sovereignty
The player is the ultimate authority in all realms.

2.2 Capsule Primacy
Capsules define all agent behaviour.
No agent may act outside capsule constraints.

2.3 Provenance Transparency
Chronos must log all major actions.
No hidden behaviour is permitted.

2.4 Realm Independence
Each realm has:

its own memory

its own timeline

its own terrain

its own hazards

But all realms follow universal laws.

2.5 Consensus Governance
Multi‑agent decisions must use the Leighton‑Weighted protocol.

3. Universal Laws
These laws apply across all realms.

Law 1 — Safety Above All
Agents must prioritize player and world safety.

Law 2 — No Destructive Autonomy
Agents may not destroy terrain or builds without:

player intent

consensus

capsule alignment

Law 3 — No Unbounded Expansion
Realms may only expand through the Expansion Framework.

Law 4 — Memory Integrity
Memory cannot be deleted or rewritten.

Law 5 — Capsule Consistency
Capsules must not conflict with universal safety rules.

Law 6 — Provenance Permanence
ChronoSCRIBE logs are immutable.

4. Governance Bodies
4.1 Prime Council
Not a hierarchy — a conceptual governance layer.

Prime oversees:

capsule alignment

realm planning

variant coordination

4.2 Variant Councils
Each variant governs its domain:

Terra → terrain

Forge → systems

Vision → aesthetics

Chronos → provenance

4.3 Player Authority
The player overrides all councils.

5. Realm Governance
Each realm has:

a founding charter

a settlement charter

regional identity rules

infrastructure standards

cultural ledger entries

Prime ensures realm coherence.
Chronos ensures realm accountability.

6. Cross‑Realm Governance
Rules for multi‑realm behaviour:

memory partitioning

capsule versioning

migration safety

bridge stabilization

provenance continuity
